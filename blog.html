Blog Post #2

In the film The Social Network Mark Zuckerberg uses his online blog to drunkenly write comments about his ex-girlfriend. Technology play a big part on writing and communication as it allows us to say and do things that we may not do face to face. As seen in a later scene when he confronts her while she is eating, he doesn’t want to say anything rude to her in person, but he was willing to do so online where he didn’t have to immediately deal with the consequences. In real life you can see evidence of people saying things that I highly doubt they would say to another person’s face. Take Youtube comments for example, because your real name can be hidden behind an online username, people feel like they can post stuff and not have to deal with the repercussions of their actions. In the real world if I made rude comments at someone I’ve never met I may get a fist in my face.

Technology also allows us to think about our comments before we say them. For example, if I was having a conversation with someone I’d probably have to come up with responses to their statements almost immediately, but online I could think for minutes to come up with a good response. I think technology is hindering how we interact with each other face to face, too often have I seen a group of people sitting at a lunch table and all of them are on their phones instead of interacting face to face. Technology has made a profound effect on how we communicate with each other, and currently I don’t think it’s for the better.
